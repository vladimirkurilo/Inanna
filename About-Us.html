We strive to provide evidence-based care and support through a team of professional and experienced midwives, nurses, and certified practitioners. Our dedicated staff is committed to providing personalized care and support, tailored to each individual’s needs and preferences. 

At Inanna Birthing Center, we believe in the power of the birthing process, and we provide our clients with the best possible care and experience throughout their journey. We specialize in water births, natural birth, unassisted birth, home birth, VBAC (vaginal birth after cesarean), and postpartum care. Our facility is equipped with state-of-the-art amenities, such as birthing tubs, birthing beds, and private rooms for labor and delivery. 

At Inanna Birthing Center, we are passionate about helping women achieve the best birth experience possible. We strive to provide a warm, caring, and nurturing atmosphere, where clients can feel safe and supported throughout their journey. We look forward to helping you create a beautiful, meaningful, and positive birthing experience!

Jean originally opened Inanna Birth &Women’s Care in 2006. She brings a total of over 39 years of nursing experience and 28 plus years of midwifery experience to the practice. Prior to midwifery, Jean worked in a high-risk Labor & Delivery unit which gave her loads of experience in what to do if problems occurred but mostly what was not necessary in most cases. She graduated from The University of Texas School of nursing in 1983 and received her Master’s degree in High Risk Perinatal Nursing in 1988. She attended Baylor College of Medicine in Houston and graduated midwifery school in 1994. She has worked in hospitals, birth centers and home births for her entire midwifery career. Her philosophy of birth is that it is usually normal if left undisturbed. She says she has lost count of all the babies she has birthed but it is somewhere in the range of 3,000. Midwifery is her greatest earthly passion, and she loves to witness how birth can change a woman’s whole life. She loves seeing the look in a mom’s eyes when she catches the first glimpse of her newborn and sees how powerful she was in birth. She is a strong Christian and uses her faith to guide her clinical decision making on a regular basis.

Jean Sala-Smith, APRN, CNM

Jean is married to Paul Smith, the love of her life. She has served as a lay biblical counselor at her church but currently co-leads a group of people from her church with her husband Paul. She is an avid gardener, bee-keeper and loves making sour dough bread. She believes that the answer to a sweet life is to live a balanced physical, emotional and spiritual life. She and her husband are in the beginning phases of opening a ministry for counseling couples who have been married before in preparation for remarriage in honor of the blessings that she and her husband have received in both their second marriages. She strongly feels that God has called her to the nursing and midwifery profession and considers caring for families a supreme privilege.
