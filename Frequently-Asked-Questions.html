1. What is a Birth Center?

A Birth Center is a State Licensed home-like facility that provides prenatal, birth, postpartum, newborn, gynecological and limited primary care for women.

2.  Is giving birth outside a hospital safe?  

 Several studies including the 2013 National Birth Center Study II and the series on midwifery Published in the Lancet, show that giving birth in a birth center is just as safe for low-risk women as low-risk women in a hospital with lower rates of premature birth, low birth weight infants, a dramatically lower cesarean section rate with higher patient satisfaction!

3.  Will a pediatrician check my baby at the birth center?   
Newborn care is also the specialty of the midwife so your baby will receive a complete head to toe examination in the first hour of life.  Your baby’s vital signs will be checked every 15-30 minutes and your baby’s oxygen level will be checked to screen for heart defects just like in the hospital. We will recheck you and your baby 3 days after birth and again at 2 weeks.  It is not necessary to take your baby to the pediatrician immediately, however, you may choose to do so.  The baby will receive the same medications and evaluations that are done in hospital.  Your baby need never leave your side during our exams!

  4.  What kind of care can I expect in a birth center?  
Our staff includes both Certified Nurse-Midwives and Certified Professional Midwives.  Each are educated in kind and compassionate, evidence-based care throughout your pregnancy and birth.  During your prenatal visits you will have plenty of time to ask questions, receive teaching about diet, exercise, and listen to any concerns that you may have. We will check your blood pressure, weight, urine, measure your tummy to see how baby is growing and listen to baby’s hear beat.  We will order an ultrasound on your first visit to check if the due date is correct and schedule a thorough anatomy scan when you are 18-20 weeks pregnant.  During labor, you will come to the birth center where the midwife on call will evaluate your progress and will admit you when your labor is progressing.  The midwife will support you, check baby’s heart beat regularly, check your vital signs regularly and offer things to help you feel more comfortable like getting into a beautiful tub to help you relax.  We also have pain medicine available if you need it.

5.  How many support people can I have during labor?
  Your midwife will be with you the entire time and a birth assistant will come in closer to time for your birth.  We don’t have any hard fast rules about how many people you can have.  After many years of catching babies, we usually recommend fewer rather than more.  Birth is a very private time for you and your partner.  Too many people can sometimes give you “stage fright” and can actually slow things down.  So, mostly how many people are at your birth is a personal choice.

6.  How do I get a birth certificate?  
On your 36 week visit, you will be given a packet of information which also includes all the information we will need to complete your birth certificate. This must be returned the following week.  We need your baby’s name before your three-day check-up.  So, start thinking about names soon!

7.  If I have a boy, do you offer circumcisions?  
 Yes, we do.  The midwife will have a long talk with you and explain about reasons why you may or may not want a circumcision.  If you do choose to have it done, we want you to wait for at least 8 days before having it done.  This gives baby plenty of time to build up his blood clotting factors to decrease the chance of bleeding  and let him learn how to nurse so he can comfort himself afterwards.  And yes, we use local anesthesia for the procedure.

8.  Do you take insurance?  
 Yes, we are in network with several different companies.  We encourage you to know your deductible and check to see if we are in network.  Most insurance companies do not pay for the facility unfortunately.  But we do offer a discount on that if you pay in full by 32 weeks.  We also have very reasonable self-pay rates!

9. What happens if I need a cesarean?
  Luckily, we are proud to say that our cesarean rate is very low!  So, you have about a 95% chance of having a vaginal delivery!  Sometimes if your labor has complications, we may need to transfer you to the hospital for care that is just not safe to do in a birth center like epidurals, Pitocin to strengthen your labor pains or any concerns with your baby.  We have physicians in the hospital that will care for you there.
